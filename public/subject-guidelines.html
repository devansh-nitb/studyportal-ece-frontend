Electronic Circuits and Devices
Mid-Semester Focus
Semiconductors & Diodes â–¼
  • Grasp the basics of semiconductors, PN junctions, and diode characteristics
  • Understand diode applications in clipping, clamping, and rectifier circuits
BJT Analysis â–¼
  • Study BJTs â€“ configurations (CE, CB, CC), biasing, and load line analysis
  • Focus on small signal analysis and AC equivalent models
  • Solve numerical problems involving designing the circuits
  • Practice drawing characteristic curves and interpreting their behavior
End-Semester Focus
Advanced Transistor Analysis â–¼
  • Understand BJT and MOSFET operation modes
  • Learn the DC and small-signal analysis of MOSFET/BJT amplifiers
  • Explore MOSFET/BJT applications in analog and digital switching circuits
Resources & Important Notes
Recommended Books â–¼
Textbook & Author: Semiconductor Physics and Devices by Donald A. Neamen
Reference: Microelectronic Circuits by Sedra and Smith
Study Strategy
NO YOUTUBE CHANNELS recommended - reading from books can only make you understand the subject in depth based upon the exam paper.
Scoring Strategy
This subject isn't just about hard work, it mainly depends on how the paper turns out. The exam usually has tough, numerical problems that you won't find directly in class notes or books. Don't expect theory or derivation-based questions - it's all about application. Focus on understanding, not just answers.

Network Analysis and Synthesis
Circuit Fundamentals
Basic Elements & Laws â–¼
  • Begin with a strong understanding of basic circuit elements â€“ R, L, C and their behavior in AC/DC circuits
  • Focus on Kirchhoff's Laws, Ohm's Law, and fundamental concepts of current and voltage division
Analysis Methods â–¼
  • Focus on Mesh and Nodal Analysis, especially for multi-loop/multi-node circuits
  • Learn and practice new cases of Thevenin's and Norton's Theorems, Millman, Superposition, and Maximum Power Transfer Theorem
Advanced Topics
Transient & Frequency Response â–¼
  • Understand transient response of RC, RL, and RLC circuits in both time and frequency domains
  • Study phasor analysis and sinusoidal steady-state behavior of circuits
  • Go through resonance, Q-factor, and bandwidth concepts in RLC circuits
Study Strategy â–¼
  • Regularly refer to class notes â€“ many direct exam questions are based on them
  • Practice a large variety of problems, especially numericals â€“ speed and accuracy are essential here
